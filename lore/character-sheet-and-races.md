### Character Races

There are plenty of species and races in Divian. In recent years, an increase in magical activity has led to much greater variety. However, with increasing tension in the world of Divian, many races have begun to stick closer together, which has caused certain traditions and personality traits to appear within each. As the world is so diverse, freedom is given to the player to make certain assumptions or decisions about their character's physical characteristics, and character personalities may differ from the race's common behaviours.

Certain races have sub-races (often with different personality traits) and/or variations (functionally the same but have differences in appearance). **Meta explanation:** Sub-races are treated as separate races, while variations simply allow for more player interpretations of the race. If a race has sub-races, you can still select the main race (for example, you can simply be a Sylvari, you don't _need_ to select a sub-race).&#x20;

The **RP Difficulty** field is simply a suggestion. It is a measure of how difficult it will be for a player to maintain a race's typical personality traits while still performing conversational and meaningful roleplay. It is possible (and common) to create a character that is either much lower or higher in RP Difficulty based on how you design their specific personality and behaviours.

When Bellfolk are unwell, the sounds of their bells become much more muted and dull. Complimenting a Bellfolk's song is deeply flattering; mimicking or mocking them is a serious insult.

This can cause complications when Fae are met with flowery or polite language (a simple "see you tomorrow" may be seen as a binding agreement to a Fae). <summary>üëπ <strong>Tiefling</strong></summary>

Tieflings are human-like creatures which often feature horns, tails, and unusual skin tones. Despite their appearance, they are not inherently evil, but they face a great deal of skepticism from other races. Many Tieflings will develop sharp social skills to navigate this, and are typically good at reading a room. This helps them in combat as well, as they are quick on their feet and excel at assessing danger. Their tails are expressive; they flick when irritated, curl when comfortable or content, and lash when angry. Perceptive observers can read a Tiefling's mood even when their face remains neutral.

* **Lifespan:** 100-150 years (child until 18)

<summary>üìú <strong>Villager</strong></summary>

Villagers are tall, long-nosed folk with a deep-rooted culture of trade, profession, and community. Every Villager is defined by their craft, which is often given to them unwillingly. Villagers are librarians, cartographers, fletchers, butchers, and more. Some villagers will even introduce themselves by their profession before providing their name. They speak in a distinctive manner, often humming or nodding or making noncommittal sounds ("hmm", "hrrm"). Interrupting or mocking these sounds is considered very rude. Villagers sleep precisely at dusk and wake at dawn, and become distressed when this schedule is disrupted. They are terrified of zombies.

* **Lifespan:** 70-90 years (child until 16)
* **Height:** 160cm-210cm
* **RP Difficulty:** ‚≠êÔ∏è
